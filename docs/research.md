## 3. UX Research Brief (Luxury Beauty Patterns)
I reviewed Chanel, Tom Ford, and Charlotte Tilbury to align our UX with premium expectations:

1. **Tom Ford Beauty:** Highly moody, heavy use of black/dark brown backgrounds. Thin borders. Typography does the heavy lifting. **Takeaway:** Minimal UI chrome, let the product imagery breathe.
2. **Chanel Beauty:** Extremely rigid grids, no rounded corners, stark contrast. **Takeaway:** Stick to 0px border-radius, it feels more editorial and timeless.
3. **Charlotte Tilbury:** Warmer, more gold accents. **Takeaway:** Use the `--color-brass` token sparingly but effectively for CTAs to guide the user's eye without feeling "cheap".

## 4. Font Pairing Validation
- `Playfair Display`: Tested and looks perfect for the cinematic display headings (italic weights especially).
- `Barlow Condensed`: Strong industrial feel, highly legible even in small caps.
